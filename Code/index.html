Pick your Game Engine

If you're going to make a game(or program of any kind) you'll have to decide what you're making it in. Ultimately that will be up to your idea, scope, and whether you're wanting to make your game in 2D or 3D or VR. The Big 3 that you'll likely hear about are in no order: Gamemaker, Unity, and Unreal Engine . I list those 3 specifically because they have extensive documentation available and have their strong suits.

  • Commonly Gamemaker is used for 2D projects and offers a great starting point for the uninitiated.
  • Unity Engine can be used for 2D, 3D, or VR and will provide a more robust engine than Gamemaker.
  • Unreal Engine can be used for 2D, 3D, or VR, it provides a potentially more indepth but more difficult to master program.
